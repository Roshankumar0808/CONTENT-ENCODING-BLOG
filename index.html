What is content encoding

Content encoding is baasically a cover sheet over an HTTP request .It is very impoetant that we do not confuse it with transfer encoding which is used to describe an encoding at a certain level.


Why should we encode our content??

Content encoding is mainly done to save the bandwidth when we encode our content we can shrink all our request enormously.The samller the request the fatser data transfer it is ,fewer bandwidth costs for your servers and lower data costs for clients and in less data

It happens automatically in many CDNs but in general it largely forgoten by many developers,that lead to unncessarily increaase in data amount due to huge transfers

What are some of the HTTP content that we can use


So here are I am adding some of the official registry of encoding,we can directly read these from there official docs some of the important one are:

identity - an encoded body, it's just raw content. Valid, but you might as well just omit the header entirely.

gzip - the standard: the content is compressed using gzip, widely used & supported almost everywhere.

br - the new kid: the content is compressed using Brotli, a new (well, 2013) compression algorithm originally used in WOFF font files. Supported in most browsers for a couple of years now, and significantly more powerful than gzip.

zstd - the very very new kid: the content is compressed using Zstandard, a compression algorithm from Facebook designed to compress better than gzip, but also especially focused on allowing much much faster decompression (at least 3.5x faster than Brotli or Gzip, according to their benchmarks).
